Gravel Liquefaction Location App Info

Gravel Liquefaction

Gravel liquefaction is an observed but less common occurance of liquefaction. liquefaction generally occurs is sandy soils. After major earthquakes it is importatn for researchers to identify locations where liquefaction has occured. As part of this identification it is also important that researchers determine the type of soil that liquified. With improved data at sites where gravel Liquefaction has ocured it is possible to better predict where it may occur in the future.

App Purpose

The purpose of this app is to provide a place where documented occurances of gravel liquefaction can be stored. The app includes a map where exisiting locations of gravel liquefaction are displayed. It also allows the user to enter new gravel liquefaction sites. The app is intended to be a resource to researchers providing location information of known gravel liquefaction sites.
